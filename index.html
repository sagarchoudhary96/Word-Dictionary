Introduction

Language is a tool to express thoughts. Sometimes a thought keeps hovering in mind but due to the paucity of an exact word we are unable to express ourselves. For that, an arranged collection of words is required through which all the words related to a thought could be depicted. A thesaurus is basically a compilation of the vocabulary of a language grouped conceptually. The concepts are divided into sub-classes, which further comprise lists of words. The words occurring in the list are inter-related. The relation amongst the words can be defined on the basis of semantic relations. The linguistic study of semantic relations incorporates synonymy, hyponymy, homonymy, polysemy, antonymy, etc. So the relation amongst the words within one group is based on such semantic relations mentioned above. Thesaurus is such a wonderful treatise which incorporates almost all semantic relations of a vocabulary. Even the instances of dimunition, shortening, slang and variant spelling are also taken care of. A glimpse into the historical perspective of the evolution of thesaurus demonstrates that India was the pace-setter. It would be appropriate here to name some representative works in the field. Nighantu, the earliest kośa of the pre-Christ days, Amar Simha's Amarkośa of the 9th century A.D. and Roget's Thesaurus of the 19th century share a genetic resemblance; they were born out of the necessity for arranging vocabulary on the basis of concepts. Generally, vocabularies are arranged in two ways: conceptual and alphabetical. Conceptual vocabularies are as old as the Vedas are. Thesauri are, in fact, conceptual vocabularies. As the aim was to prepare a thesaurus in mother tongue, the significance of light in language and culture is dealt with elaborately. 'A Thesaurus of Punjabi Words and Phrases on the Concepts of Light' boils down ultimately arranging Punjabi words and phrases relating to the phenomenon of light within the parameters of a thesaurus. Light is obviously recognized as an essence of life, both physical and metaphysical. There are, thus, 71 categories in all. This number happens to be almost at par with that of the relevant groups salvaged out of the different classes in Roget's Thesaurus of 1960 edition. The list is a thesaurus of words relating to light. One can comfortably choose appropriate words to suit one's ideas with minimum effort to avoid frustration or irrelevance. The present study incorporates the semantic basis of vocabulary network and conceptual framework exhaustively. Such 'basics' are likely to equip the thesaurus-makers with the wealth of ideas, linguistically valid, in the accomplishment of task, they undertake. The work suffers from certain limitations. The model, might serve the purpose partly as the vocabulary of a language extends beyond the phenomenon of light. A comprehensive thesaurus of Punjabi is a dire need of the hour. Framework of a Thesaurus of Light: The vocabulary of this book is transliterated in Roman script, in order to break the language barrier for the non Punjabi readers. The list of the symbol is given in the beginning to facilitate the readers for easy reference. The work is divided into two main parts i.e. Text and Index. In Text, approximately three thousand words related to the concept of light are conceptually arranged. Light, in Text, is divided into three aspects. Light(in general) Physical: sources of light: both natural and artificial, life, splendour, colour, heat and faculty of seeing. Metaphysical: piety, knowledge, truth, goodness, spiritualism and super naturalism. These are subdivided into fourteen conceptual headings which are further sub divided into seventy one categories. For example, under the metaphysical aspect comes Supernaturalism, which further has four categories: supernaturalism (in general), sorcery, spirits, mysticism and the nether world. Under these categories, the wordlists are arranged on the basis of sense relations. In index, all the words categorised in the text are alphabetically arranged. The arrangement is based on Roman script to facilitate the non-Punjabi readers for easy reference. The category number of the concept in which that particular word occurs is given along with to figure out the place and associations of that word with other words.
